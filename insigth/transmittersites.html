Transmitter Sites Tower Inspections

2 October, 2018

Tower Inspections Check List:

Preventative maintenance at FM Broadcast transmission sites must include appropriate risk management practices.

Therefore it is highly recommended that a structural and equipment inspection be done annually and after any severe weather to ensure continued high availability of the Broadcast Services.

The following is what is included in the standard transmitter site inspection for towers.

Photos will be taken of key details and will be kept for future use to track changes over time that might require attention for risk mitigation.

Schedule:

Annual Inspections Severe Weather and Natural Disasters. Following significant weather and disaster events.

Self Supporting Tower Inspection:

  • Visual inspection of overall condition of the tower from ground

  • Measurement of base flange heights to verify that tower is plumb

  • Visual inspection of Civil Aviation lighting for proper function

  • Verify operation of photo cell switch

  • Visual inspection of safety climb if applicable

  • Visual inspection of overall tower condition noting any potential problems

  • Visual inspection of antennas and transmission lines

  • Visual inspection of paint condition if applicable

  • Equipment environment operating temperature

Equipment Shelter inspection:

  • Visual inspection of equipment connections

  • Visual inspection of RF equipment performance compliance

  • Identify equipment and performance changes

  • Identify Equipment operating condition

  • System grounding inspection

  • Backup power system condition inspection

  • Equipment environment operating temperature

  • Visual inspection of overall site condition

Other Included Inspection Items:

  • Broadcast Antenna Inspections

  • Broadcast Line Pressure Checks

  • Modulation performance confirmation

  • Check Radio Frequency Interference Status
